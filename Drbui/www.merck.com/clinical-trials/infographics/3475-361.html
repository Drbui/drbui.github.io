A Phase III, Randomized, Unblinded, Open-label, Controlled Clinical Trial of Pembrolizumab with or without Platinum-Based Combination Chemotherapy versus Chemotherapy in Subjects with Advanced or Metastatic Urothelial Carcinoma (MK-3475 361)

At a Glance

This clinical research study will evaluate the investigational study drug, pembrolizumab, or MK-3475, as a treatment in advanced bladder/urothelial cancer. Pembrolizumab has been approved for use in certain patients with melanoma, non-small cell lung cancer and head and neck squamous cell cancer, but it has not been approved for advanced or metastatic bladder/urothelial cancer.

  • Condition: Bladder/Urothelial Cancer
  • Study Treatment/Method (drugs): Pembrolizumab as a single treatment, pembrolizumab combined with standard treatment, or standard treatment alone
    • Phase: III
    • Enrollment Goal: 990
  • Possibility of Active Study Treatment:

Clinical Study Approach

WHAT WE'LL NEED FROM PARTICIPANTS:

A new or archived sample of tissue from any tumor site and blood tests

Radiographic imaging every 9-12 weeks

Study visits every 3 weeks

  • Other requirements as decided by the study doctor.

The Study Drug

The study drug, MK-3475, also known as pembrolizumab, is an investigational drug for bladder/urothelial cancer. Pembrolizumab has been approved for use in certain patients with melanoma, non-small cell lung cancer and head and neck squamous cell cancer, but it has not been approved for advanced or metastatic bladder/urothelial cancer.

Participation & Eligibility

DURATION & REQUIREMENTS SUMMARY

You must have a diagnosis of advanced or metastatic bladder/urothelial cancer. The study will last approximately 36 months.

 

Study Locations

We are planning on conducting this study in the United States and/or Canada.
