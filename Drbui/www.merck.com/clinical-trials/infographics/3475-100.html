At a Glance

This clinical research study will evaluate the investigational study drug, pembrolizumab, or MK-3475, as a treatment for advanced recurrent ovarian cancer. Pembrolizumab has been approved for use in some patients with melanoma and non-small cell lung cancer, but it has not been approved for advanced recurrent ovarian cancer.

  • Condition: Advanced Recurrent Ovarian Cancer
  • Study Treatment/Method (drugs): Pembrolizumab
    • Phase: II
    • Enrollment Goal: 325
  • Possibility of Treatment with pembrolizumab:

Important Information to Consider

There's a lot to think about when considering whether to volunteer for a clinical study. Any clinical study includes risks, which the study doctor will review with you. Make sure you understand the risks before deciding to participate.

Also, this is not a complete overview of study criteria. A study doctor will review your information to see if you qualify.

To learn more about how clinical studies work, visit the Patients & Caregiver section of the Merck Clinical Trials site, as well as our frequently asked questions.

Clinical Study Approach

WHAT WE'LL NEED FROM PARTICIPANTS:

A sample of your tissue from any tumor site and blood tests

Radiographic imaging about every 9 to 12 weeks

Study visits every 3 weeks

  • Other requirements as decided by the study doctor.

The Study Drug

The study drug, MK-3475, also known as pembrolizumab, is an investigational drug for advanced recurrent ovarian cancer. Pembrolizumab has been approved for use in some patients with melanoma and certain patients with non-small cell lung cancer, but it has not been approved for advanced recurrent ovarian cancer.

Participation & Eligibility

DURATION & REQUIREMENTS SUMMARY

You must have a diagnosis of advanced recurrent ovarian cancer. The study will last approximately 48 months.

 

Study Locations

We are planning on conducting this study in the United States and/or Canada.
